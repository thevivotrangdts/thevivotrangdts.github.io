<h3>What Challenges Do Coffee Businesses Face When <span>Implementing Carbon Labels</span>?</h3>
</div>

<div class="content">

<img src="/assets/img/product/giai-phap-gan-nhan-carbon-ca-phe/carbon-label-coffee-2.png" alt="Challenges in implementing coffee carbon labels" class="responsive" style="max-width: 800px; width: 100%; display: block; margin: 0 auto 24px;">

<p>Although demand for <strong>carbon labels for exported coffee</strong> is growing, practical implementation still faces many barriers, especially for small and medium enterprises.</p>

<p>The first challenge is <strong>lack of input data</strong>. Most raw material areas have not been digitized, farming information is fragmented, making emission data collection costly and inaccurate.</p>

<p>Second is the <strong>difficulty in calculating carbon footprint</strong>. International methods such as IPCC or ISO require standardized processes and high technical expertise, beyond the capabilities of many businesses.</p>

<p>In addition, the issue of <strong>traceability</strong> is also a bottleneck. Without a transparent system, emission data will be difficult for international partners to accept.</p>

<p>Finally, consulting and certification costs are currently high, making widespread implementation difficult to achieve.</p>

<p>For this reason, the current trend is to move toward technology-integrated solutions that automate and reduce implementation costs.</p>
